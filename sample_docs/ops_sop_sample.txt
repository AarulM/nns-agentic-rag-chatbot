OPERATIONS STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (SAMPLE / SYNTHETIC DOCUMENT)

1. Hull Section Quality Check
- Upon completion of a hull section weld, the assigned Quality Assurance (QA) inspector performs a visual weld inspection followed by a non-destructive test (NDT): either ultrasonic testing (UT) or radiographic testing (RT), per the applicable weld class.
- Discrepancies are logged as a Nonconformance Report (NCR) in SMAX and routed to the Engineering Liaison for disposition.
- A section may not move to the next production stage ("erection") until all open NCRs on it are closed or dispositioned "use as is."

2. Material Staging and Scheduling
- Steel and outfitting material must be staged at the work area no later than 24 hours before the scheduled start of the task, per the master production schedule.
- Schedule conflicts or material shortages should be reported by opening an Operations ticket in SMAX with priority "high" and tagging the Production Control team.

3. Shift Handoff
- Each shift lead documents open work, safety concerns, and equipment status in the shift log before handoff.
- Critical path delays must be communicated to the next shift lead directly (in person or via Jabber) in addition to the written log.

4. Equipment Downtime
- Equipment failures affecting the production schedule are reported immediately via SMAX ticket, category "equipment," priority based on schedule impact.
- Maintenance provides an estimated time to repair (ETR) within 2 hours of ticket creation.

5. Change Requests
- Any deviation from the approved build plan requires a Engineering Change Request (ECR), reviewed by Engineering and Quality before implementation.
